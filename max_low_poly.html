Low Poly Portrait

Project Description

This is a piece which I completed as part of a larger project for Graphic Design I in Fall 2024. We were assigned to create a low-poly portrait - a portrait made up of triangles - of either ourselves or our pet. I immediately knew which option I would choose; my childhood dog, Max, passed away about a year ago now, and I wanted to honor his memory. This piece is based on a photo I took of him the day I went back to college sophomore year.

First, I used the pen tool in Adobe Illustrator to create a mesh of triangles. I utilized the original image as a reference, and I created triangles based on any blocks of color which I noticed. Then, I applied the Live Paint and eyedropper tools to color match each triangleâ€™s color with the reference image. Although the image is technically low-poly, I wanted to be as detailed as possible - I was aiming for a Seurat-like effect, in which you saw the image of my dog from far away, but the closer that you got, the more you noticed each individual triangle.

Lastly, to represent the concept of simultaneous contrast, I created two renditions of the same portrait with different-colored backgrounds: one that is blue and one that is orange. I chose these colors because I was aiming for a contrast between warm and cool.

Out of all of my graphic design work, this is one of the pieces which I am most proud of. It means a lot to me personally, and I worked really hard to bring it to life.
