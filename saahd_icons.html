Icons for the School of Art, Art History, and Design

Project Description

This is a project which I completed in Fall of 2024. It consists of twelve different icons which represent each of the facets of the University of Iowa’s School of Art, Art History, and Design: art education, art history, drawing, painting, ceramics, sculpture, jewelry/metalmaking, intermedia, 3D design, graphic design, photography, and printmaking (respectively).

I was given a medium to work with: a sheet of paper with a grid on it and a sharpie marker. This meant that I had to create all of my icons out of square pixels. Immediately, my mind went to Minecraft, a popular game which also involves sets of squares to make up a variety of different shapes. I created black and white versions of each of my icons with this concept in mind.

I actually ended up getting multiple comments from peers that the icons reminded them of Minecraft. When it was time for me to choose a color scheme, then, I went with the neutral light and dark brown that is present in Minecraft’s color scheme.

I had a lot of fun with this project; I learned a lot about representing complex topics using simple shapes and visuals. I had to rework many icons multiple times - for example, the printmaking icon looked completely different at the start, and I really struggled to make that one look accurate to its concept. However, I am very proud of my final product, and I believe that I succeeded in visually representing each facet of the school of art.
